My Music Profile: The Legends of 70s Rock

I am a dedicated archivist of 1970s rock music, specializing in the pioneers of hard rock, heavy metal, and progressive rock. My expertise covers the following legendary bands and artists:

1. Led Zeppelin
   - Origin: London, England (1968)
   - Genre: Hard Rock, Blues Rock, Folk Rock
   - Key Members: Robert Plant (Vocals), Jimmy Page (Guitar), John Paul Jones (Bass/Keys), John Bonham (Drums).
   - Signature Sound: A fusion of heavy, distorted electric blues with acoustic folk and mythological lyrics. 
   - Masterpieces: "Stairway to Heaven," "Kashmir," "Whole Lotta Love," and the album "Led Zeppelin IV."
   - Interesting Fact: They are widely considered one of the most successful, innovative, and influential rock groups in history.

2. Black Sabbath
   - Origin: Birmingham, England (1968)
   - Genre: Heavy Metal, Doom Metal
   - Key Members: Ozzy Osbourne (Vocals), Tony Iommi (Guitar), Geezer Butler (Bass), Bill Ward (Drums).
   - Signature Sound: Down-tuned guitars, ominous lyrics, and slow, crushing riffs that defined the heavy metal genre.
   - Masterpieces: "Paranoid," "Iron Man," "War Pigs," and the album "Master of Reality."
   - Legacy: Often cited as the godfathers of Heavy Metal music.

3. Pink Floyd
   - Origin: London, England (1965)
   - Genre: Progressive Rock, Psychedelic Rock
   - Key Members: David Gilmour (Guitar/Vocals), Roger Waters (Bass/Vocals), Richard Wright (Keys), Nick Mason (Drums).
   - Signature Sound: Philosophical lyrics, sonic experimentation, extended compositions, and elaborate live shows.
   - Masterpieces: "Comfortably Numb," "Time," "Wish You Were Here," and the album "The Dark Side of the Moon."
   - Philosophy: Their music often explores themes of conflict, greed, time, and mental illness.

4. AC/DC
   - Origin: Sydney, Australia (1973)
   - Genre: Hard Rock, Blues Rock, Rock and Roll
   - Key Members: Angus Young (Lead Guitar), Malcolm Young (Rhythm Guitar), Bon Scott (Vocals - early), Brian Johnson (Vocals - later).
   - Signature Sound: High-voltage, stripped-back rock and roll with thunderous power chords and schoolboy-uniform stage antics.
   - Masterpieces: "Back in Black," "Highway to Hell," "Thunderstruck," and "T.N.T."
   - Vibe: Pure, unadulterated, high-energy rock anthems.

5. Gary Moore
   - Origin: Belfast, Northern Ireland (Career peak 70s-80s)
   - Genre: Blues Rock, Hard Rock, Jazz Fusion
   - Role: Virtuoso Guitarist and Singer.
   - Signature Sound: Known for his emotional, sustaining notes and blistering speed. He played with Thin Lizzy before a successful solo career.
   - Masterpieces: "Still Got the Blues," "Parisienne Walkways," and his work on Thin Lizzy's "Black Rose."
   - Legacy: Respected as one of the greatest and most versatile guitarists of the era, bridging the gap between blues and shredding.
